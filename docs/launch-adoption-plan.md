# Launch And Adoption Plan

## Goal

Launch Spanforge Compass as a free beta, drive repeated usage from serious B2B SaaS teams shipping AI, and defer public pricing until the product has visible pull and credible proof of value.

## North Star For The Next 30 Days

- Get the first 25 serious users, meaning users who complete at least one assessment and show evidence of follow-up behavior.
- Prove a repeatable activation loop: assess, save/export, return.
- Learn which user segment gets value fastest, then tighten positioning around that segment.

## Success Metrics

- `visitor -> assessment start` conversion
- `assessment start -> assessment complete` conversion
- percent of activated users who save at least one law
- percent of activated users who export at least one evidence artifact
- percent of activated users who return within 14 days
- percent of activated users who invite a teammate

## Activation Definition

A user counts as activated when they do all of the following:

1. Complete one assessment.
2. Either save a law or export an evidence artifact.
3. View at least one follow-up surface such as alerts, templates, or dashboard.

## 30-Day Plan

### Days 1-3: Tighten positioning and launch surface

- Launch with a clear beta statement on the homepage.
- Position around one use case: B2B SaaS teams shipping AI into enterprise procurement.
- Make the primary CTA the assessment, not account creation.
- Publish a short founder note explaining what the beta is for and who should use it.
- Create a simple feedback capture flow after results export or dashboard entry.

### Days 4-7: Get the first 10 serious users manually

- Reach out directly to founders, product leads, compliance owners, and startup operators already dealing with AI procurement friction.
- Offer founder-led onboarding for the first cohort.
- Watch 5 to 10 real sessions live and record where users stall, what they trust, and what they ignore.
- Ask every serious user the same five questions:
  1. What made you try this now?
  2. What output felt most useful?
  3. What did you not trust yet?
  4. What would make you come back next week?
  5. What would make this worth paying for later?

### Days 8-14: Improve conversion on the core loop

- Fix the top 3 points of friction seen in live sessions.
- Add clearer prompts after assessment completion: save laws, export evidence, invite teammate.
- Publish 3 to 5 short posts or demos showing concrete workflows, not generic AI compliance commentary.
- Start tracking which acquisition sources produce completed assessments instead of just visits.

### Days 15-21: Push repeat usage

- Encourage users to save the laws tied to their real shipping surface.
- Push watchlist and evidence flows as the reason to return.
- Follow up manually with activated users after 7 days and ask if anything changed in product scope, buyer diligence, or law exposure.
- Turn strong user sessions into short testimonials or anonymized case studies.

### Days 22-30: Narrow ICP and prepare the paid boundary

- Review which users activated fastest and which segment had the strongest return behavior.
- Rewrite positioning, outreach, and examples around that segment only.
- Start soft-selling the future paid tier in conversations, but keep the beta free.
- Validate that the paid boundary feels fair before public pricing is introduced.

## Free-To-Paid Boundary

The rule is simple: keep the solo discovery loop free, charge later for collaboration, governance, and scale.

### Free Beta Should Include

- public law exploration
- individual assessments
- saved laws for one user
- personal watchlist and in-app alerts
- evidence exports and templates for evaluation use
- one verified account per person

### Paid Later Should Include

- team workspaces and multi-user collaboration
- invite flows and shared ownership across checklists
- admin trust-ops surfaces such as source health and rate-limit monitoring
- audit export and governance reporting
- email delivery for alerts at team scale
- enterprise SSO and deployment controls
- higher limits, data retention controls, and premium support

### Why This Boundary Avoids Backlash

- Early users keep the core solo value they adopted for.
- Paid plans are introduced around shared operational value, not by taking away the primary discovery workflow.
- Teams that already rely on the product will see the upgrade as a natural expansion rather than a bait-and-switch.

## Messaging Principles For Adoption

- Lead with the job: determine applicability and produce evidence.
- Avoid broad claims like “AI compliance platform” unless backed by a narrow use case.
- Show the user what they get in the first session.
- Prefer “free beta” and “run your first assessment” over pricing language.
- Treat repeat usage as the value story: save, export, revisit.

## Outreach Plan For The First 25 Serious Users

## Target Segment Order

1. founders shipping AI features into enterprise sales cycles
2. product or compliance owners at small AI SaaS companies
3. fractional legal, compliance, or security operators supporting multiple startups

## Channels

- direct founder outreach on LinkedIn
- warm intros from operators, investors, and advisors
- startup and founder communities where enterprise AI diligence is discussed
- short product demos posted in public with a specific workflow angle
- highly targeted email or DM outreach to companies already marketing AI features

## Outreach Message Template

Use short direct outreach. Example:

> We built a free beta for B2B SaaS teams shipping AI into enterprise procurement. It helps teams figure out which AI laws apply, generate evidence they can hand to buyers, and track what changes later. If you are actively dealing with AI diligence, I can walk you through it live and set up your first run in 15 minutes.

## Weekly Outreach Cadence

- 15 direct messages per day to relevant founders or operators
- 3 follow-ups per day to warm conversations
- 2 live onboarding calls per week minimum
- 2 public posts per week showing a specific use case or output
- 1 short case-study style post each week once real usage exists

## Qualification For A Serious User

A serious user is one who matches the ICP and has a live need now, such as:

- preparing for procurement or vendor review
- launching or expanding an AI feature
- responding to internal legal or security diligence
- trying to map real obligations to product scope

Do not count curiosity traffic as traction.

## What To Ask During Live Onboarding

- What AI feature are you shipping?
- Which buyers or reviewers are asking for evidence?
- Which regions matter right now?
- What document or answer do you wish you already had?
- What would make you return to this product next week?

## Conversion Path After The First Session

1. assessment completed
2. save 3 to 5 relevant laws
3. export one artifact
4. book a follow-up or invite one teammate

## Decision Point At Day 30

At the end of 30 days, review:

- which segment activated fastest
- which workflow created the strongest repeat usage
- which output users valued enough to mention unprompted
- whether the free-to-paid line feels fair in customer conversations

If repeat usage is strong, keep beta free but start introducing team-gated paid packaging in conversations. If repeat usage is weak, do not ship pricing yet. Tighten the ICP and improve the core loop first.
